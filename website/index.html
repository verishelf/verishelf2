How Store Teams Use VeriShelf

A day-in-the-life view of how VeriShelf fits into real store operations, from shift start to end-of-day summary.

1

Start of Shift: Prioritized Action List

Associates open VeriShelf on their phone and immediately see a prioritized list of expiring and issue items, organized by aisle and shelf location. No guessing, no manual lists. The system shows what needs attention right now, sorted by urgency.

2

On the Floor: Quick Status Updates

As associates work through the list, they mark items as removed, discounted, or re-merchandised with a simple tap. The mobile app works offline, so even in dead zones, updates sync automatically when connection returns. Photos and notes are captured instantly for the audit trail.

3

Manager Mid-Shift: Monitor Progress & Exceptions

Managers check the dashboard mid-shift to see real-time progress across all aisles. They focus on exceptions—items that need attention, locations falling behind, or unexpected issues. No need to walk the floor asking for updates; the system shows exactly what's happening.

4

End of Day: Automatic Summary

At shift end, managers receive an automatic summary email with key metrics: items handled, compliance status, and any outstanding issues. No manual reporting required. Regional and corporate teams see the same data across all locations, giving operations leadership reliable visibility without extra work.

Frequently Asked Questions

Operational questions about how VeriShelf works in real store environments.

How do store teams actually interact with VeriShelf?

Associates open VeriShelf on their phone at the start of shift and see a prioritized list of expiring items organized by aisle and shelf. They work through the list, marking items as removed, discounted, or re-merchandised with simple taps. The app works offline and syncs automatically when connection returns.

What devices are supported?

VeriShelf works on any device with a web browser: smartphones (iOS and Android), tablets, and desktop computers. The mobile experience is optimized for on-floor use, while managers and regional teams use the full dashboard on any device. No special hardware or apps to install.

What happens if a check is missed?

The system runs automated expiry checks every 15 minutes and sends alerts for expired items. If an item isn't handled, alerts escalate and managers see it in their exception view. The audit trail shows exactly when items were flagged and when they were addressed, providing full accountability.

How is data tracked and reviewed?

Every action is logged with timestamp, user, location, and photos. Managers see real-time progress in the dashboard. At shift end, automatic summaries are generated and shared with regional and corporate teams. No manual reporting required—all data is audit-ready and accessible through the dashboard.

What are the integration expectations?

VeriShelf can integrate with POS and ERP systems via API to reduce manual data entry. Integration is optional—the system works standalone. For Enterprise plans, we provide API access and dedicated support for custom integrations. Most stores start with manual entry and add integrations as needed.
